ADITL
[1] get ahold of a digital camera. [2] carry it with you for twenty-four hours. [3] capture an entire day in images.

ADITL (a Day in the Life of MIT) is a photographic collaboration seeking to tell the story of a single day at MIT through as many cameras as possible. The idea is simple: on September 24, 2009, you carry a digital camera with you for twenty-four hours (midnight to midnight) and photograph your day. Document the world around you — shower tiles, secret classroom yawns, the way the light hits the student center steps, eight straight hours of staring at an emacs buffer, whatever it is you see in your day. Upload your photos following the event to aditl.mit.edu, where we will create a space to browse and view the collective day.

Registration is open now. Stay tuned. ADITL is managed and funded by Technique, the Yearbook and Photography Club of MIT.
