Frequently Asked Questions

* Are there any restrictions on the pictures I can submit?

The pictures you submit must have been taken on April 21 only. That is, anytime between the start of April 21 at midnight to the end of April 21 the following midnight.

Your digital camera must have a clock, and the clock must be set correctly. The reason for this is that the website will take advantage of the embedded exif timestamps in the pictures you take. We will provide a test page where you can veify that your camera's clock is set correctly. We also ask that you set your camera to take pictures as jpegs (as opposed to TIF or RAW format if your camera can do that). Don't worry, we'll remind you again of these requirements via email before the event.

Other than that, there are no restrictions. Take pictures of anything, in any way you want during those 24 hours.

* But I'm not a good photographer!

We're not looking for "artsy" photography. We want to know how you spend your day. Everyone's daily life is unique, and we want to get as many points of view as possible. Don't worry about it! Oh yeah, if you want to submit "artsy" photography, that's fine too.

* I don't have time!

We're not asking you to go out of your way to take pictures. Just carry your camera around with you, and occasionally pull it out and shoot a picture. Whether you're in class, or walking down the infinite, or at lunch, or on the toilet, or laying in bed watching tv.

* Do I have to use a digital camera? Can I submit film?

The ADITL web site depends on knowing when you took your pictures. If you're willing to keep an accurate account of when you took pictures throughout the day and are willing to scan your film and submit the scans, then we'll be happy to accept your submission. We cannot accept developed or undeveloped film (we tried this last time--it didn't work); you must scan your film yourself and submit the scans. If you're interested in this, contact aditl@mit.edu. You'll still need to register. Mad props if you go this route and use a Holga or Lomo.
